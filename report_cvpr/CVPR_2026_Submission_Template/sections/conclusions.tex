\section{Conclusion}

This project explored reinforcement learning for continuous control and 
sim-to-real transfer across two tasks. In Part 1, the Actor-Critic algorithm 
equipped with Generalized Advantage Estimation, advantage normalization, 
and learning-rate decay proved to be the strongest performer on the Hopper 
environment, while vanilla REINFORCE and its constant-baseline variant
highlighted the role of variance reduction in policy gradient methods.

In Part 2, the focus shifted to sim-to-real transfer in the PandaPush environment.
The final selected approach was SAC combined with UDR. Training on a broad distribution of cube masses produced
a more robust policy that reduced the sim-to-real gap, confirming the effectiveness of domain randomization as a
practical strategy for improving transfer performance in robotic reinforcement-learning tasks.
